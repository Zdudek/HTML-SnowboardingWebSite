History of Snowboarding

        The development of snowboarding was inspired by skateboarding, sledding, surfing and skiing. It was all started when boys and men would attach plywood or wooden planks from barrels to their feet using clotheslines and horse reins in order to steer themselves down hills. Modern snowboarding began in 1965 when Sherman Poppen, an engineer in Muskegon, Michigan, invented a toy for his daughter by fastening two skies together and attaching a rope to the end so she would have some control as she stood on the board and glided downhill. Dubbed the "Snurfer". The person that made the first board that we are familiar with today is Jake Burton Carpenter, a Vermont native who enjoyed snurfing since the age of 14, impressed the crowd at a Michigan snurfing competition with bindings he had designed to secure his feet to the board. In 1977, that same year, he founded Burton Snowboards in Londonderry, Vermont. The "snowboards" were made of wooden planks that were flexible and had water ski foot traps. Burton eventually becoming the biggest name in the business, coming right out of Vermont!
